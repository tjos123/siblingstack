Most "best double stroller" roundups are written for twins — same size, same seat needs. If you've got a newborn and a toddler instead, the stroller has a harder job: one seat needs a near-flat recline, the other needs to fit a toddler who wants to face forward and watch the world.

*Prices, stock, and exact specs shift constantly — treat the numbers below as a starting point and confirm current details on the retailer page before you buy.*

## Quick picks

- **Best overall for mixed ages:** UPPAbaby Vista V3 with Rumbleseat
- **Best lightweight option:** UPPAbaby Minu Duo or Nuna TRVL Double
- **Best budget side-by-side:** Mompush Lithe
- **Best for car-seat-to-stroller transitions:** Nuna TRVL Double (PIPA-series compatible, no adapter needed)

## UPPAbaby Vista V3 with Rumbleseat V2+

The closest thing to a purpose-built solution for your situation. The Vista's main seat handles a true newborn (bassinet attachment available), while the Rumbleseat add-on creates a second, independently reclining seat for an older sibling — the two don't have to be on the same recline angle or used the same way at the same time. One of the few systems explicitly designed to add a second seat after you already own the single stroller, which matters if you bought the Vista for your first kid and are now adding a sibling.

**Worth knowing:** it's a heavier, bulkier system than a true lightweight double — this is the stroller for daily walks and errands, not the one you check at airport gates.

## UPPAbaby Minu Duo

If you want something genuinely portable, the Minu Duo trades some of the Vista's flexibility for size. It folds compactly and is light enough to lift in and out of a trunk repeatedly without it being a workout. Suits families doing mostly short outings rather than half-day excursions.

## Nuna TRVL Double

Each seat independently reclines and is rated from birth up to 50 lbs. Notably, it accepts any PIPA-series infant car seat directly, without a separate adapter. If you're already using or considering a Nuna infant seat, this removes one extra purchase. At roughly 25 lbs, it folds one-handed.

## Mompush Lithe

The budget-conscious pick that still gets the fundamentals right: independent recline on both seats, roughly 25 lbs, and a one-step fold. Narrower than many side-by-sides, which matters if you're regularly navigating standard doorways. Won't have the plush ride quality of the premium options, but covers the basics.

## What to check before buying any of these

- **Per-seat weight limit, not the combined total** — marketing often leads with a combined number that doesn't tell you what either individual seat can hold.
- **Whether the infant seat attaches directly or needs an adapter** — an adapter is an extra cost and an extra thing to lose.
- **Your actual trunk space and front door width, measured** — side-by-side doubles are wider than they look in product photos.
